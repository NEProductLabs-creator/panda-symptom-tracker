Build out the /right-now route as a crisis-support pathway for parents in the middle of a first flare.

Right Now landing page is a single calm screen. Heading: "We are right here with you." One short parent-to-parent paragraph below. Then four sub-route cards:

1. "It's the PANDAS, not your child" at /right-now/reframe
   Three to four short paragraphs in second person to the parent. Explain that severe OCD, rage, and regression during a flare are driven by inflammation in the brain. Reinforce that the child the parent knows is still there. Use soft mist tone for callout quotes.

2. "What to do today" at /right-now/today
   Checklist for the next 24 hours:
   - Write down what you are seeing right now (date, time, behaviors)
   - Take a short video if you can, especially of unusual movements or tics
   - Note any recent illness, sore throat, or strep exposure in the past four to six weeks
   - Call your pediatrician tomorrow and ask for a strep test, throat culture, and ASO and Anti-DNase B titers
   - Tell the pediatrician: "I am concerned about PANS or PANDAS. The change was sudden."
   Each item is tappable to mark complete. State persists per user per day.

3. "De-escalation in the moment" at /right-now/de-escalation
   Two collapsible sections.
   OCD spirals: do not argue with the compulsion, lower the stakes, name the PANDAS out loud, reduce sensory input.
   Rage episodes: safety first, low stimulation, do not match volume, wait for the wave to pass.
   Tone is calm and grounded.

4. "Should we go to the ER?" at /right-now/er-guide
   "Go now if" criteria: suicidal statements with intent, inability to eat or drink for 24 or more hours, severe psychotic symptoms, dangerous self-harm.
   What to bring: symptom log printout, list of recent illnesses, current medications.
   What to say at intake: "My child had a sudden neuropsychiatric change. I am concerned about PANS or PANDAS, which can mimic a psychiatric emergency. Please consider an infectious workup including strep titers."
