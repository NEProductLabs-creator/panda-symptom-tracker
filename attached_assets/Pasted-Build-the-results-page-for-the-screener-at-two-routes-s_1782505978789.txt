Build the results page for the screener at two routes:

- /screener/results (anonymous, reads from React Router state or 
  sessionStorage)
- /app/screener/results/:id (authenticated, reads from Supabase by id)

Both routes render the same ResultsPage component. The component 
accepts answers and resultBucket as props, and a mode prop matching 
the wizard's modes.

Layout (same structure for all three result buckets):

1. Persistent top banner: yellow background, warning icon, text "This 
   is a screening tool, not a medical diagnosis. Only a licensed 
   healthcare provider can diagnose PANS or PANDAS."

2. Title and subtitle, vary by bucket:

   strong_match:
   - Title: "Your answers match the pattern doctors look for in PANS 
     or PANDAS."
   - Subtitle: "We strongly recommend bringing this summary to your 
     child's doctor. If your pediatrician is not familiar with PANS or 
     PANDAS, the PPN practitioner directory can help you find one who 
     is."

   partial_match:
   - Title: "Some features match, others don't."
   - Subtitle: "Worth a conversation with your pediatrician, especially 
     if symptoms get worse."

   no_match:
   - Title: "These symptoms don't fit the typical PANS/PANDAS picture."
   - Subtitle: "That doesn't mean nothing is wrong. If you're worried, 
     talk to your pediatrician."

3. Section "What you told us": a formatted summary table of the 
   parent's answers grouped by Onset, Core symptoms, Other symptoms, 
   Recent illness, Other diagnoses. Each section has an "Edit" link 
   that opens the wizard back at that step.

4. Section "Why this matches" (strong_match only) or "What matched 
   and what didn't" (partial_match only). Omit entirely for no_match. 
   Three to four plain-language bullets explaining which criteria 
   were and weren't met.

5. Section "What to do next": numbered action list, varies by bucket.

   strong_match:
   1. Download the PDF and bring it to your child's next appointment.
   2. If you don't have an appointment, schedule one this week.
   3. Find a PANS/PANDAS-aware doctor in the PPN directory.
   4. Track daily symptoms in the app so your doctor sees a timeline.
   5. Visit the Care Hub for what to expect and questions to ask.

   partial_match:
   1. Download the PDF and keep it for reference.
   2. Watch for new symptoms or worsening over the next few weeks.
   3. If concerned, schedule a pediatrician visit.
   4. Track daily symptoms in the app to build a clear picture.

   no_match:
   1. Explore other resources in the Care Hub.
   2. If you're concerned, talk to your pediatrician.

6. CTAs at the bottom:
   - Primary: "Download PDF for your doctor"
   - For anonymous mode only, a second primary: "Save and track over 
     time" linking to the signup flow. Card copy above the button: 
     "Save this to your child's profile so you can track symptoms 
     over time and update this screening as things change. Free, 
     takes 30 seconds."
   - Secondary link: "Find a PANS/PANDAS specialist" linking to 
     https://www.pandasppn.org/practitioners
   - Tertiary: "Retake screener" (calls back to /screener or 
     /app/screener)

7. Optional expander (anonymous mode only), placed just above the 
   Download PDF button:
   - Toggle label: "Add child's name and date of birth to the PDF"
   - When expanded, two fields: child's first name (text, optional) 
     and date of birth (date picker, optional)
   - Small helper text under the fields: "This stays on your device. 
     We don't receive or store this information."
   - CRITICAL: these fields must never leave the browser. No fetch, 
     no POST, no analytics payload, no logging may include these 
     values. They are passed directly into the client-side PDF 
     generator in the next prompt.

8. Footer line:
   - Authenticated mode: "You can retake this screener anytime as 
     symptoms change. Your answers are saved to [child name]'s 
     profile."
   - Anonymous mode: "You can retake this screener anytime. Your 
     answers are not stored on our servers."

Analytics events (no personal data in payloads):
- results_page_viewed (result_bucket, mode)
- edit_section_clicked (section, mode)
- pdf_download_clicked (result_bucket, mode, included_name boolean, 
  included_dob boolean) - the booleans only, never the values
- signup_cta_clicked_from_results (anonymous only)
- find_specialist_clicked

Do not include any treatment information anywhere on the page. The 
words antibiotic, steroid, IVIG, plasmapheresis, and NSAID should not 
appear.

PDF generation is the next prompt. For now the Download button can 
console.log the payload it would pass to the generator.
